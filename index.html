We're proud to announce that applications are open for the 2014 Knight-Mozilla Fellowships. If you're someone who likes to code and you're interested in spending 10 months working with some of the best newsrooms in the world creating innovative approaches to the news, now is the time to apply!

What is OpenNews?

OpenNews is a collaboration between the Knight Foundation and Mozilla that's dedicated to building an ecosystem to help journalism thrive on the open web. It's about producing next-generation web solutions that solve real problems in news. It's about supporting communities of developers and journalists as they make, learn, and invent together. And it's about deploying fellows—and code—into news organizations to collaborate and innovate in new ways.

Fellowships

Knight-Mozilla Fellows spend 10 months embedded in partner newsrooms. They are [paid](benefitslink) to work with the community inside and outside of their newsroom to develop and share open-source projects that, [in a variety of ways](communityoffellowslink OR http://source.mozillaopennews.org/en-US/organizations/opennews/), help to transform journalism on the web. Learn more.

Hack days

We sponsor hack days across the globe to write code and build community and momentum around creating technical solutions to problems that working journalists face. Get involved.

Source

We run Source as a centerpoint for the code being written throughout the journalism community. From features that give the context behind the code, to an index of the community making it, Source is dedicated capturing the people and projects behind journalism code. Visit now.
